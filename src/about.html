Nice to meet you!

I’m Nina Le (She/Her), a third-year design student at York University and a UX/UI designer pursuing an Honours Bachelor of Design. I’m passionate about creating thoughtful, inclusive solutions that optimize feasibility, viability, and desirability for current or future features. Whether working on UX/UI or experimental projects, I love diving into research and collaborating with cross-functional teams to craft experiences that truly cater to the user.

My attention to detail—shaped by years in choir and traditional/digital illustration—fuels my approach to design. Combined with my industry experience, this foundation drives my focus on accessibility, precision, and creating work that feels both engaging and impactful.

My Design Values

Responsible Design

Applying human-centered design principles to decision-making, ensuring solutions are ethical, inclusive, and considerate of their impact on all stakeholders. Prioritizing accessibility, equity, and sustainability while aligning with broader business goals.

Fanciful Utility

Crafting designs that balance delight and usability, integrating meaningful embellishments to enhance aesthetic usability without compromising the functionality or user flow—like creating a seamless rhythm that captivates and guides the user.

Precision in Intuition

Combining micro-interactions, hierarchy, and meticulous attention to detail to craft intuitive experiences that feel effortless yet intentional. Focusing on iterative design to ensure designs evolve based on research and user feedback while delivering exceptional consistency and usability.
